Security

Closed an exposure where internal tools were reachable from the open internet RISK REMOVED
During a routine audit, we found close to 300 old internal utility tools — leftover from early system setup and one-off data fixes — that were sitting in a part of the website anyone could reach directly, without logging in. A handful of them were capable of changing data directly in the database. There is no evidence they were misused, but the exposure was real. All of them have now been moved out of reach and locked down, while the system continues to work exactly as before.
✅

System Reliability — Money-Handling Made Safer

Payroll, invoices, warehouse records, and other financial processes now protect themselves against interruptions STRENGTHENED
Several processes that update multiple records at once — for example, paying an employee and posting it to the books, or invoicing against a certificate — were not fully protected against something going wrong halfway through (a network hiccup, a server restart, and so on). In the worst case this could have produced duplicate invoices or half-completed payroll runs. We have now made these processes "all-or-nothing": either the entire update completes correctly, or none of it does, and the record stays clean. This protection now covers payroll, invoicing, warehouse stock removal, and several sales/CRM update flows.
Added an automatic daily check on all financial reports NEW SAFEGUARD
The system now automatically verifies, every time a change is made, that the Trial Balance, Income Statement, Balance Sheet, and Cash Flow all still add up correctly and agree with each other. Any discrepancy would be caught immediately rather than being discovered later during a review.
📦

New Feature

Customer Purchase Order (LPO) workflow, from request to invoice LAUNCHED
Customer purchase orders previously lived as a basic note attached to a customer's profile, with no formal review or approval step. This has been rebuilt into a complete workflow: a customer order is raised, reviewed, and approved by the right level of authority, then flows straight through to a delivery note and finally to an invoice — all connected, so nothing has to be re-typed and every step is traceable back to where it started. The Sales menu was also reorganised so the order of these steps is easier to follow.
🔧

Fixes & Corrections

Payroll processing was failing for every employee FIXED
A recent update to the payroll calculations had an oversight that caused every "Process Payroll" run to fail outright, for every employee, with an error. This has been corrected and payroll now processes normally.
Sales/CRM dashboard was showing blank charts and tables stuck on "Loading" FIXED
The dashboard used by the sales team to track leads, activity, and top performers was failing to display its charts and tables for some users. Also fixed the bulk customer/lead upload tool, which was not accessible and had a few rough edges once reachable.
A release-process issue was blocking updates from reaching the live system FIXED
An automated safety check in our release process was out of date after the new Customer Order feature was built, and was refusing to let updates go live. This has been corrected so releases flow through normally again.
Warehouse selection was inconsistent across a few screens FIXED
A small number of screens were allowing a warehouse to be selected that didn't belong to the project being worked on, which could have led to stock being recorded against the wrong location. This is now consistent everywhere.
Several smaller corrections FIXED
Company document numbering now updates correctly on existing records when the numbering format is changed. Delivery note options now only appear on the correct screens (Purchases vs. Sales), avoiding confusion. The unit of measure on a product (kg, litres, pieces, etc.) can no longer be accidentally changed after the product is selected on an order or invoice.
